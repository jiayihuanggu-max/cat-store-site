Care Tips for First-Time Cat Owners

Building Trust & Routine

Patience: Allow your cat to adjust to the new home at its own pace.

association: Build trust by associating yourself with positive experiences, like offering treats or playing with interactive toys.

Consistent routines: Maintain steady feeding times, play sessions, and a consistent litter box location to help your cat feel secure.

Daily play: Engage in daily playtime with your cat to fulfill their physical activity and mental stimulation needs.

Litter & Hygiene

1 litter box per cat + 1 extra; scoop daily to reduce odor.

Enrichment

Scratching posts and cat trees: These provide a natural place for cats to scratch, trim their claws, and serve as a jungle gym for exercise and napping.

Toys: Keep your cat mentally stimulated and physically active with a variety of toys, such as puzzle toys, feather toys, and interactive toys like a laser pointer or a feather wand.

Health & Safety

Veterinary care: Schedule regular veterinary visits for vaccinations, checkups, and microchipping.

Spay/Neuter: Consider having your cat spayed or neutered to reduce behavioral issues and prevent unwanted pregnancies.

Cat-proofing: Cat-proof your home by hiding electrical cords, securing breakable items, closing toilet lids, and putting human food away.

ID Tag: Even for indoor cats, provide a collar with an ID tag including your phone number.

Grooming

Brush weekly (more for long hair); introduce nail trims slowly.

Travel & Stress

Use carriers with soft bedding; practice short rides first.
